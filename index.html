Once upon a spreadsheet, in the land of UNAM’s equations, a young economist found delight in chasing patterns, not vacations. Armed with questions (too many to count), and a laptop full of dreams, she wandered from Mexico’s numbers to London’s data streams. Now a Master of Applied Social Data Science, LSE-certified and proudly caffeinated, she writes code like others write letters, debugging her destiny, semi-colonated. She believes life, much like R, works best with better settings, so she keeps upgrading reality, while answering questions worth forgetting. Soon to embark on her PhD quest, she polishes her mind in this digital nest, seeking skill, curiosity, and colleagues who dare, to ask big questions — and actually care.
